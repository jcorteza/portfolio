About Me

Josephine Cortez-Ayala

I discovered a passion for web-development purely by accident while finishing a dual major in psychology and communications. I took a computer science class for non-majors in order to fulfill a credit for graduation—not anticipating that degree to which I would enjoy the class dubbed baby-python, i.e. Jython.

When I'm coding, I gain a clear sense of satisfaction. It fulfills the need for creativity I sought to fulfill through the field of communications. Web development is also challenging, requiring logical reasoning, which suits my persistent (stubborn) nature, and I've found my background in psychology and customer service suit the field of web development from a UX standpoint.

I’ve recently completed a full-stack web-development certification sponsored by UT Austin and am in search of my first web-development opportunity. In the meantime, I’ll be learning new skills, reading up on upcoming trends in the field, and coding side projects.
